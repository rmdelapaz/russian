🇷🇺 Lesson 8: Shopping & Money

🎯 What You'll Learn

From giant shopping malls to bustling open-air markets, shopping in Russia has its own vocabulary and culture. This lesson covers money, prices, clothing, the genitive case with numbers, and essential shopping phrases.

Estimated Time: 45–60 minutes

✅ Grammar Note: Rubles and the Genitive Case

Remember the number-noun rule from Lesson 5? Here it is with rubles:

1 рубль (rubl') · 2 рубля (rublya) · 5 рублей (rublyey)

1 копейка · 2 копейки · 5 копеек

"Сколько стоит?" (How much does it cost?) → "Триста рублей" (300 rubles)

💡 Cultural Insight: At Russian markets (рынок), especially for food and souvenirs, bargaining is acceptable and even expected. However, in regular shops and malls (торговый центр), prices are fixed. Russia has embraced contactless payment — most places accept cards, and apps like Мир Pay are widely used. The Russian payment card system "Мир" (meaning "world" and "peace") has become dominant for domestic transactions.

🗣️ Practice Dialogue

— Здравствуйте! Сколько стоит эта куртка?

(Zdrastvuytye! Skol'ka stoit eta kurtka?) — Hello! How much is this jacket?

— Пять тысяч рублей.

(Pyat' tysyach rublyey.) — Five thousand rubles.

— Можно примерить?

(Mozhna primyerit'?) — Can I try it on?

— Конечно! Примерочная там.

(Kanyeshna! Primyerachnaya tam.) — Of course! The fitting room is over there.

— Мне подходит. Я возьму. Вы принимаете карты?

(Mnye padkhodit. Ya vaz'mu. Vy prinimayetye karty?) — It fits. I'll take it. Do you accept cards?

— Да, конечно.

(Da, kanyeshna.) — Yes, of course.
