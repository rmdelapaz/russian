🇷🇺 Lesson 9: Directions & Transportation

🎯 What You'll Learn

Russia is vast — navigating its cities and intercity transport is essential. This lesson covers direction words, the Moscow Metro, trains, taxis, and the prepositional case for locations.

💡 Cultural Insight: The Moscow Metro is not just transportation — it's an underground palace. Opened in 1935, many stations feature chandeliers, marble columns, mosaics, and sculptures. Stations like Комсомольская, Маяковская, and Площадь Революции are architectural masterpieces. Russians are proud of their metro and visitors often ride it just for the beauty. St. Petersburg's metro is the deepest in the world, with some stations 86 meters underground!

✅ Grammar Note: Prepositional Case for Location

To say where something is located, use в (in) or на (on/at) + prepositional case:

в магазине (v magazinye) — in the store

в метро (v metro) — in the metro (метро doesn't decline)

на улице (na ulitsye) — on the street

на станции (na stantsii) — at the station

Basic endings: masculine/neuter -е; feminine -е or -и

🗣️ Practice Dialogue

— Извините, как пройти к Красной площади?

(Izvinitye, kak prayti k Krasnay ploshchadi?) — Excuse me, how do I get to Red Square?

— Идите прямо, потом налево. Это пять минут.

(Iditye pryama, patom nalyeva. Eta pyat' minut.) — Go straight, then left. It's five minutes.

— А метро далеко?

(A metro daliko?) — And is the metro far?

— Нет, станция "Охотный Ряд" рядом, направо.

(Nyet, stantsiya "Akhotniy Ryad" ryadam, naprava.) — No, "Okhotny Ryad" station is nearby, to the right.

— Спасибо большое!

(Spasiba bal'shoye!) — Thank you very much!
